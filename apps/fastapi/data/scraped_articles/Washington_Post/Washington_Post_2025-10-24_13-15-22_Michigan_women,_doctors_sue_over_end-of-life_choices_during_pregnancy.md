As an emergency medicine physician, Viktoria Koskenoja has seen families struggle with deciding how to care for loved ones incapacitated by illness or injury.

Those encounters drove her to draft an advance directive for her own care if a time came when she could no longer make those decisions herself. It includes her wish to be removed from life support if she were ever incapacitated with no hope of recovery, even if she were pregnant.
